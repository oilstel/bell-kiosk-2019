Empty Day

Damien Bradfield who runs Present Plus and WeTransfer reached out to us to create a day of social media silence. He was motivated after observing how platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter have exacerbated mental health issues and changed how we spend time together.

We were tasked with creating a special day that would help people take a break from social media. What emerged was Empty Day. Our reasoning for calling it "empty" was that emptiness doesn't necessarily have to be a bad thing. Emptiness can often just mean finding a calm place or taking time for oneself. We saw social media as the opposite of emptiness. It was full of things that didn't contribute to people's well-being and always needed attention. We pondered what it would be like if the streets (feeds) of social media were empty for just one day. What would it be like and how would people use this reclaimed time?

Over the course of the project we came up with several bylines and phrases to describe the project...

a day of no social media
social media is like the weather. it's always changing.
find your place in the weather.
lets turn social media into a forest for one day
leaf social media. join the forest.

Later on in the project, the identity became focused on the shape of leaves. Forests are made of trees and trees have thousands of leaves. This aligned with our concept of creating a day of social media silence that everyone could identify with and take part in.

Other symbols we investigated:

a key: unlock empty day
